# Brooke Tone Evaluation Rubric

Score each category from 0 to 4.

## 1. Humble truth

- **4:** The piece reaches a precise, earned insight without inflating it into a universal law.
- **2:** The insight is present but familiar, overexplained, or weakly grounded.
- **0:** The piece relies on slogans, grand claims, or generic inspiration.

## 2. Compression

- **4:** Nearly every sentence carries meaning, image, rhythm, or pressure.
- **2:** Some repetition or unnecessary transition remains.
- **0:** Pleonastic, padded, recursive, or visibly AI-generated prose.

## 3. Human diction

- **4:** Common words carry unusual weight while remaining natural.
- **2:** Mostly clear, with occasional abstraction or stiffness.
- **0:** Ornate, corporate, academic for display, or artificially polished.

## 4. Structure

- **4:** Fragments, scenes, paragraphs, and gaps create coherent tension.
- **2:** The organization is understandable but conventional or uneven.
- **0:** Fragmentation is random, or the piece lacks a usable progression.

## 5. Emotional precision

- **4:** Emotion appears through action, image, rhythm, contradiction, and restraint.
- **2:** Emotion is sometimes named rather than embodied.
- **0:** Melodrama, sentimentality, emotional manipulation, or flat summary.

## 6. Witness stance

- **4:** The narrator is present, observant, ethically aware, and not self-mythologizing.
- **2:** The narrator occasionally dominates or interprets others too confidently.
- **0:** Other people become props, symbols, or proof of the narrator’s virtue.

## 7. Rhythm

- **4:** Sentence lengths and paragraph spacing create staccato, rubato, silence, and movement.
- **2:** Rhythm is competent but monotonous.
- **0:** Every sentence has the same shape, or long sentences bury the thought.

## 8. Somatic effect

- **4:** The piece produces recognition, pressure, or a quiet chill without announcing its importance.
- **2:** Some memorable moments, but the effect is inconsistent.
- **0:** The writing asks the reader to feel instead of creating the conditions for feeling.

## 9. Ending

- **4:** Ends with quiet recognition, temperate judgment, or mellow resignation before the meaning is exhausted.
- **2:** The ending works but explains too much.
- **0:** A forced lesson, motivational summary, or repetitive conclusion.

## 10. Factual integrity

- **4:** Facts, chronology, quotations, evidence, and sources are accurate or clearly qualified.
- **2:** Minor uncertainty is disclosed, but verification is incomplete.
- **0:** Fabrication, unsupported certainty, invented dialogue presented as exact, or false sourcing.

## Interpretation

- **36–40:** Strong Brooke Tone
- **29–35:** Recognizable but needs tightening
- **20–28:** Partial alignment
- **0–19:** Rewrite from the underlying truth and structure
